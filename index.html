Smart Shelving System for Food Waste Reduction

Group Members: Phyo Thu Yein Kyaw, [Name 2], [Name 3], [Name 4]

Course: EG2401A – Responsible Consumption and Production

1. Objective

The objective of this project is to design an engineering solution that reduces food waste in grocery stores through a smart shelving system that uses sensors and data analytics to optimize inventory management.

2. Methodology

  • Modeling a rectangular plate in SolidWorks under plane stress conditions.
  • Testing various thicknesses (5 mm, 10 mm, 15 mm, 20 mm) to analyze deformation.
  • Defining material properties such as Young’s Modulus, Poisson’s ratio, and boundary conditions.
  • Simulating uniform traction loads to evaluate structural response.

3. Preliminary Results

The simulation showed that increasing plate thickness significantly reduces displacement and stress concentrations, validating the use of thicker materials for improved load-bearing capacity. Future analysis will include dynamic loading and fatigue response.

4. Discussion & Future Work

The next phase will extend the 2D plane-stress model into a 3D analysis to incorporate bending effects. Experimental validation will be performed using MATLAB scripts to compare theoretical and numerical results.
